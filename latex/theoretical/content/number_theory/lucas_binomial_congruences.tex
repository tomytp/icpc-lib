\subsection{Lucas's Theorem on Binomial Congruences}

Let $p$ be a prime number. Then, for integers $n, m \geq 0$ with base-$p$ expansions

$$n = n_0 + n_1 p + n_2 p^2 + \cdots + n_k p^k, \quad
m = m_0 + m_1 p + m_2 p^2 + \cdots + m_k p^k,$$

the following congruence holds:

$$
\binom{n}{m} \equiv \prod_{i=0}^k \binom{n_i}{m_i} \pmod{p}.
$$
